Last summer, I joined a small group tour to explore a historic city. Our guide was a friendly local woman named Maria. She showed us the most famous landmark in the city — a large stone bridge that was over five hundred years old.

In the morning, we walked through the old town. The streets were narrow and charming. I picked up a small pottery bowl as a souvenir for my mother. The shop owner showed us how to make it by hand.

After lunch, I checked my itinerary and saw that we had a museum visit planned. We needed to exchange some currency first, so we stopped at a local bank.

At the end of the day, we went through customs at the border. I was tired but very happy. I always make a reservation in advance on my trips, so everything went smoothly.
