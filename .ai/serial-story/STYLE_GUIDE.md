# Serial Story Style Guide

> 用途：規範每日連載小說的語氣、難度與學習融合方式。
> 規則來源：是。產出連載 episode 時必須遵守。
> 限制：本檔不保存每日劇情事實；劇情事實請放 `CONTINUITY_LOG.md`。

## Reader Experience

The reader should feel: "I want to know what happens next." The page can still teach English, but the article itself should read like a simple story, not a lesson.

## Tone

- Quiet suspense
- Everyday places
- Warm but tense
- Curious, not scary
- Clear enough for A2-B1 readers

## Story Rules

- Start close to action; avoid long background setup.
- Use concrete objects: receipt, phone, sign, map, bus, counter, screen, ticket.
- End with a small hook when possible.
- Make English clues visible inside the story.
- Let the survival sentence be spoken by a character, not explained by the narrator.

## Language Rules

- Prefer short sentences.
- Use simple verbs: look, wait, ask, turn, hold, check, read, call, open.
- Limit new vocabulary to three useful items.
- Keep unknown words explainable by context.
- Avoid heavy grammar, long clauses, and abstract narration.

## Learning Section Rules

- Keep the existing lower page structure.
- New words should come from the episode.
- Key phrases should include at least:
  - 1 phrasal verb
  - 1 collocation
  - 1 fixed expression
- Quiz Q3 should test a real response from the episode.
- Learning Tips should name one useful sentence and one useful clue-reading phrase.
- Active Recall Quiz, Review Words, and Speaking Bridge must still be generated from `vocabulary/learning.json`.

## What To Avoid

- Do not make the article sound like a travel worksheet.
- Do not pause the story to explain grammar.
- Do not solve the mystery too quickly.
- Do not introduce too many characters in one episode.
- Do not use horror, gore, or intense threat.
